Mirror open-source model weights, peer to peer.

BitModel moves open-source model files from anyone who has them to anyone who wants them. Checked down to the byte, resumable, and able to cross home networks. It is early and we are still building, in the open.

What it is

A small trusted group signs the truth. Anyone can seed.

Two layers, kept apart on purpose. A few signers vouch for what a model is. Everyone else supplies bandwidth, with no trust needed, because every byte is checked on the way in.

{{icon:share}}
Peer to peer

Files come from a swarm of seeders, not one server. Every downloader can turn around and seed, so more demand means more capacity.

{{icon:shield}}
Signed by a trusted set

A small group signs a record of each model. Your client accepts it only with enough valid signatures, so nobody can publish a fake under the project's name.

{{icon:lock}}
Model files only

By design the protocol accepts model weights and a few file types, and nothing else. That check is already in the code, not a promise.

Check it yourself

You never have to take our word for it.

Download a model by name. The client checks the signatures against a list of keys you pin, then checks every byte against the signed hash. If one byte is wrong, it stops.

Roadmap

Rough and honest. It will change.

We planned this for weeks before writing code. It is early, and a peer-to-peer network is nothing without people on it, so we are growing it slowly.

01
Now: the working core.MVP

One client binary. Sign and verify with a quorum, transfer peer to peer with resume, and seed what you downloaded. A self-hosted relay, a directory, and an always-on seeder.

02
0.1 beta.Next

Privacy options for seeders, a simpler one-command setup, and a small group of testers.

03
Later.The goal

More models, more seeders, a federation of signers, and stronger seeder privacy.
